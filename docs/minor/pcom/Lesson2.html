Lesson 2: Types of Communication

Learning Outcomes

  1. Demonstrate knowledge about the role of language in human communication;
  2. Identify the types of communication in relation to communication mode, context and purpose and style;
  3. Differentiate verbal, non-verbal, and visual communication and their sub-forms;
  4. Identify the differences among interpersonal, extended, organizational, intercultural communication;
  5. Explain the differences between formal and informal communication;
  6. Discuss the advantages and disadvantages of different communication types.

What is Communication

Communication

The exchange of thoughts, ideas, concepts, and views between and among two or more people.

Context

The circumstance or environment in which communication takes place, which may include the following: the physical or actual setting, the value positions of a speaker/listener, and the relevance or appropriateness of a message conveyed.

Classifications of Communication

    According to:
  1. Communication Mode
  2. Context
  3. Purpose and Style

  1. Types of communication according to MODE

    1. Verbal and Non-verbal
      • The blending of these two types is effective in communication.
      • Rather than just speaking, it is effective to also use the non-verbal code through a handshake, an approving facial expression, and a kind of disposition or character.
    2. Visual Communication
      • It uses visuals to convey information and/or messages.
      • Examples are signs, symbols, imagery, maps, graphs, chart, diagrams, pictograms, photos, drawings or illustrations and even various forms of electronic communication.

  2. Types of communication according to CONTEXT

    1. Intrapersonal Communication
      • The latin prefix 'intra-' means within or inside .
      • It means talking to oneself.
      • Some label it as self or inner talk, inner monologue, or inner dialogue.
    2. Interpersonal communication
      • As opposed to 'intra' , the latin prefix 'inter-' means between , among , and together .
      • An interactive exchange takes place.
      • May occur in dyads or small groups.
      • Interpersonal
        • A communication situation if it is meant to establish or deepen one's relationship with others.
      • Transactional
        • A communication situation where the objective is to achieve something at the end of the conversation.
    3. Extended Communication
      • It involves the use of electronic media.
      • It includes tele, audio, or phone conferencing, video conferencing, Skype calls, etc.
      • Public in nature.
    4. Organizational communication
      • Communication plays in organizational context.
      • Organizations comprise individuals who work for the company.
      • A system of communication should be put in place for an organization to be successfull.

      Four approaches of formal structure

      1. Downward Communication
        • Flows from upper to lower positions.
      2. Upward Communication
        • Bottom-up.
        • Subordinates send communications to their superior/boses.
      3. Horizontal Communication
        • Lateral in approach.
        • Takes place among people belonging to the same level but coming from different departments or units.
      4. Crosswire Communication
        • Diagonal in nature.
        • Employees from different units or departments working at various levels communicate with each other.

      Organizational Communication

      • Organizational Culture
        • The culture of each organization.
        • Based on the organization's history and development.
        • Develop its own core values, vision and mission statements, goals, and objectives.
    5. Intercultural Communication
      • Communication between or among people having different linguistic, religious, ethnic, social, and professional backgrounds.
      • It is important not to be judgemental or to rush into interpretations as cultures sometimes vary enormously.

  3. Types of communication according to PURPOSE AND STYLE

    1. Formal Communication
      • Employs formal language delivered orally or in written forms.
      • To inform, to entertain, and to persuade.
    2. Informal Communication
      • Does not employ formal language.
      • Involves personal and ordinary conversations with friends family members or acquaintances.
